FAQ

Questions RV owners ask

Straight answers about mobile RV repair — before you pick up the phone.

Do you really come to me?

Yes — that's the whole point. Home driveway, RV storage lot, or campground. Tools and common parts come on the truck.

What kinds of RVs do you work on?

Class A, Class C, travel trailers, fifth wheels, and toy haulers. We do not service pop-up campers.

What's the most common reason people call?

AC not cooling, roof leaks, slide-outs sticking, electrical issues, and appliance problems (fridge, water heater) top the list.

My AC is running but not blowing cold air — what's wrong?

Usually one of three things: low refrigerant, a failing compressor, or a dirty/blocked condenser coil restricting airflow. A tech needs to diagnose on-site to know which. See our RV AC repair page for more.

My fridge isn't cooling but it's not the AC — what could it be?

RV fridges run on a separate cooling system (absorption or 12V compressor) from your AC — totally different unit. Common causes: a failed cooling unit, blocked vents, or the fridge not being level. See our appliance repair page.

Do you do roof repairs or leak resealing?

Yes — roof leaks and reseals are one of our most common calls. That covers lap sealant and full reseals, skylight/vent/AC gasket leaks, soft spots, and delamination, on EPDM, TPO, and fiberglass roofs. Most leaking rigs are sitting in storage when the damage happens, so we inspect and reseal right at your storage lot — no towing a leaking roof down the freeway. See our roof repair page for the full rundown.

What does a mobile visit cost?

A service call fee covers travel and diagnosis, plus parts and labor for the repair. Pricing is upfront when you call — before anyone is dispatched.

Do I need to be there while you work?

Depends on the job — some repairs need you there to approve findings, others don't. If your RV lives in a storage lot, give us the gate details and the tech handles the rest.

How fast can someone come out?

Mobile RV repair typically schedules in days rather than the four to eight weeks dealer service departments commonly quote in Southern California. Call with your city and the symptoms and you'll get realistic timing for your repair — no promises we can't keep.

Do you handle emergencies or same-day breakdowns?

If your AC dies in the summer heat or you've got an active leak, tell us it's urgent when you call and we'll do what we can to fit you in.

Do you work on engines, transmissions, or towing?

No — we handle the RV's living systems: AC, electrical, plumbing, appliances, slide-outs, and roofs.

What if my repair needs a shop?

Some jobs — collision work, full roof replacement, frame repair — belong in a bay. When that's the honest answer, you'll be told that straight, and pointed toward a trusted local independent shop instead of a dealer queue.

Will you work on warranty or insurance claims?

Extended warranty and insurance claims are common in this business. Mention it on the call and it gets handled the right way from the start.

My RV lives in a storage lot — is that a problem?

It's a specialty. The Inland Empire is full of RV storage, and dragging a rig out to fix a fridge makes no sense. Give us the gate details and the tech handles the rest.

Do I need to buy the parts, or do you bring them?

Common parts and consumables come on the truck. If your repair needs a special-order part, you'll be told that on-site before any work starts, along with realistic timing.

What areas do you serve?

Riverside County and the surrounding corridor — home, storage lot, or campsite. See our full list of service areas.

How often should I get my RV serviced?

At least once a year, or before and after a long trip. The Inland Empire sun is hard on roof sealant especially — an annual roof check costs little, while a hidden leak can cost a wall, a floor, and thousands in water damage.
